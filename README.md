# AIAA DBF 2026: What I Learned as Team Lead & Chief Engineer

## In One Sentence
I led a team of 12 to design, build, and fly an aircraft that carried 3 ducks and 1 hockey puck, deployed a 4-meter banner, and competed against 97 universities worldwide — finishing 32nd in the flyoff.

But this repository is **not** about the rank. It's about what I learned when things went wrong.

---

## Who I Am (The Short Version)

I'm an engineering student who:
- **Loves complex systems** — the kind where one wrong decision ripples through everything
- **Thrives under pressure** — I discovered that I think clearest when everyone else is panicking
- **Takes ownership** — when my teammate said “we can't finish Mission 1”, I ignored him and kept working. We finished it.

I'm not the loudest person in the room. But I'm the one who:
- Catches the rule change everyone missed
- Builds the framework so no one has to “remember” anything
- Takes the blame when my team fails — and gives the credit when they succeed

## The One Thing I Want You to Remember

We had perfect test flights. Everyone was confident. Then Mission 3 failed — and I lost my confidence for weeks.

I later realized: the failure wasn't in our design. It was in our **system**.

I had built a team that relied on heroes, not redundancy. I didn't have AB角 backups. I forgot our own pressure test because it wasn't built into our habits.

**That mistake will never happen again.**

And that — not the 32nd place — is what I'm most proud to share.
