# AIAA-DBF-2026-Team-Lead-and-Chief-Engineer-

## Overview:
This repository introduces my roles, contributions, design methodology, testing and results of AIAA DBF 2026 competition which also served as my final year project. In AIAA DBF 2026, we designed an aircraft that can fly in an average velocity of 32m/s, carrying 3 plastic ducks as passengers and 1 hockey puck as cargo in mission 2, and deploying a banner with a length of 4 meter and as aspect ratio of 4.95. We ranked 28th in report round, and 32nd out of 98 teams from universities worldwide in the flyoff (actual flight mission contest) in Textron Aviation Employee's Flying Club, Wichita, Kansas, USA from 16th to 19th April 2026. 

## My roles:
### Team Leader:
- Designed systematic framework for the team to monitor technical progress
- Arranged logistics for the team to perform flight tests in Hong Kong Mechanical Engineering Club
- Allocated financial resources for manufacturing materials, secured academic and industry sponsorships from Innovation Academy, Faculty of Engineering, the University of Hong Kong, Department of Mechanical Engineering, University of Hong Kong, Ansys software for CFD simulation, Financial Subsidies from L'Voyage, a private jet company in Hong Kong, and from the Safety Collaborative, a human factor consultancy in Hong Kong. The total fundings received summed up to 109,000+ HKD.
- Designed and led 30+ workshops and recruitment talks for DBF new members to guide and share knowledges in aircraft designs, CAD modelling on SolidWorks, Aerodynamic analysis on XFlr5 and CFD analysis on SolidWorks and Ansys, and 3D printing from year 2024 Fall to 2025 Fall.
- Designed team's jacket, posters for DBF 2025 and DBF 2026.
- Organized and arranged gatherings and meetings between supervisors and AIAA DBF members, and non-final year members.

### Chief Engineer:
- Designed specifications for main wing and conducted aerodynamic analysis and stability analysis (both static and dynamics) for our aircraft.
- Conducted CFD analysis to examine the static and dynamic pressure distribution of the aircraft in laminar flow simulation to provide suggestions for the best prototype for competition performance. 
- Designed a Multidisciplinary Optimization Framework (MDO) that includes all the data obtained from propulsion analysis, aerodynamic analysis, conceptual design and predict the best score case the aircraft under the design requirements and constraints determined by AIAA DBF 2026 rules.
- Designed, manufactured and tested 5 types of banner mechanisms to successfully stow, deploy, tow and release the banner during flight.
